THERMAL CONDUCTIVITY VALUES REFERENCE

For inclusion in the Monte Carlo Heat Diffusion Report:

The thermal conductivity values used for the physics correction (T ∝ 1/κ) were obtained from:

CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics, 104th Edition (2023-2024), 
Section 12: Properties of Solids, Table "Thermal Conductivity of Metals and Alloys"

This is the standard reference for material properties in engineering and physics applications. All values are reported at room temperature (20-25°C).

Material Properties Used:
- Silver: 429 W/m·K
- Gold: 318 W/m·K  
- Copper: 401 W/m·K
- Aluminum: 237 W/m·K
- Iron: 80.4 W/m·K
- Steel, Carbon (AISI 1010): 54 W/m·K
- Steel, Stainless (304A): 16.2 W/m·K

Citation Format:
Rumble, J.R., Ed. (2023). CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics, 104th Edition. 
CRC Press/Taylor & Francis, Boca Raton, FL.
